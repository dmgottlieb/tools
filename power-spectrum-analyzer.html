Radial-Averaged Power Spectrum

What is this? The radial power spectrum shows the distribution of spatial frequencies in an image. High values at low frequencies indicate large-scale features, while high values at high frequencies indicate fine details and edges. The radial average provides a rotationally-invariant measure of frequency content.
⚠️ Phase Matters! These reconstructions demonstrate the critical importance of phase:
  • Full Reconstruction: Uses both magnitude and phase → perfect recovery of the original
  • Zero Phase: Uses magnitude with all phases set to zero → produces the autocorrelation function
  • Random Phase: Uses magnitude with randomized phases → produces noise-like static
